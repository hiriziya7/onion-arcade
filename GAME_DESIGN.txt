================================================================================
ONION ARCADE — GAME & ECONOMY DESIGN (current state)
================================================================================
Purpose: a self-contained description of how the arcade works today — the games,
the onion economy, player incentives, how the devs get paid, and the prize-pool
mechanics — so it can be handed to an AI (or a person) as the basis for a
redesign. No codebase access is assumed; everything needed is below.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. WHAT IT IS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
A neon, 8-bit web arcade of short skill/reflex games. Players compete on
per-game leaderboards. Playing costs "onions" (a real currency on the OnionDAO
platform). The onions players spend accumulate into a prize pool that is paid
out to top players; the operator (devs) takes a 10% cut of everything spent.

The arcade runs in two modes:
  - LOCAL mode (no OnionDAO configured): a sandbox. Players get a free local
    grant and onions are fake/local. Used for dev/testing.
  - CONNECTED mode (OnionDAO configured): the real economy described here. Onions
    are real, backed by an OnionDAO escrow "wallet" the arcade owns.
Everything below describes CONNECTED mode unless noted.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. THE GAMES (3 today)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
All are single-player, score-per-round, and feed a per-game leaderboard. Each
round costs a fixed number of onions (see GAME_COST). "Lower is better" means a
smaller score ranks higher.

  a) SEVEN          (id: seven)        — score: "ms off", LOWER is better
     Stop a hidden clock at exactly 7.000 seconds. No visible timer. Score is how
     many milliseconds you were off from 7.000s. Pure internal-clock skill.

  b) LIGHTS OUT     (id: lights-out)   — score: "ms", LOWER is better
     F1-style reaction test. Five red lights illuminate, then go dark; react the
     instant darkness hits. Score is reaction time in ms. Jumping the start
     (reacting before lights-out) is penalized.

  c) ONION CHOP     (id: onion-chop)   — score: "pts", HIGHER is better
     A blade sweeps across the board; tap to chop at dead-center. 20 swings per
     round; hitting center builds a combo multiplier for a higher score.

LEADERBOARD RULES
  - Each game has its own leaderboard. A player's leaderboard entry is their BEST
    single score (MIN for lower-is-better games, MAX otherwise).
  - Ties broken by who achieved it first (earliest timestamp).
  - Every round's score is recorded; personal best is shown to the player.
  - Identity on the leaderboard = the player's OnionDAO username (see section 3).

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. IDENTITY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  - CONNECTED mode: a player signs in with their real OnionDAO USERNAME. It is
    validated against OnionDAO. That username is how they appear on leaderboards
    and is the wallet their onions move to/from.
  - LOCAL mode: a player picks a local "@id" handle (no real account).
  - One identity gate covers the whole arcade on first visit.
  - NOTE (current limitation): username entry is not authenticated — a visitor
    can type any existing OnionDAO username to appear under it on the
    leaderboard. Funds are still safe (deposits require the real user to approve
    in their own OnionDAO portal), but leaderboard identity can be spoofed. A
    redesign should consider real OnionDAO login (magic-link / badge).

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. THE ONION CURRENCY & THE ARCADE WALLET
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  - "Onions" are the OnionDAO platform currency. They can be POINTS (web-only) or
    TOKENS (held on a physical OnionDAO badge) — the platform decides per user;
    the arcade never chooses.
  - The arcade owns ONE shared OnionDAO "escrow account" = the arcade WALLET. All
    real onions live here. The arcade can pull onions OUT of this wallet to any
    user with NO approval (it holds the wallet's secret) — this is what makes
    instant payouts/cash-outs possible.
  - Inside the arcade, each player has a local "credit" balance (a ticket). 1
    onion deposited = 1 credit. Credits are what the games actually spend. The
    real onions sit in the shared wallet; the credit ledger is the arcade's
    promise of what it owes each player back.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5. PLAYER MONEY FLOW (the core loop)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  STEP 1 — BUY IN (one approval):
    Player chooses an amount (presets 25 / 50 / 100, or custom 1–100,000) and
    approves ONCE in their OnionDAO portal/badge. Those onions move into the
    arcade wallet, and the player is credited the same number of local credits.
    (In CONNECTED mode players start at 0 — no free onions — so they must buy in
    to play.)

  STEP 2 — PLAY (no approvals):
    Each round costs GAME_COST credits (currently 5), deducted instantly from the
    local balance. No per-round approval, no waiting. A player can play as many
    rounds as their balance allows.

  STEP 3 — CASH OUT (anytime, instant):
    A "Cash out" button returns the player's ENTIRE UNUSED balance from the
    arcade wallet back to their OnionDAO wallet. Instant for points users; for
    badge/token users it may settle asynchronously ("sent — confirming…").
    Onions already SPENT on games are NOT refundable — they're gone into the
    prize pool. So a player effectively pays only for what they played.
    (Current rule: cash-out is all-or-nothing — the whole remaining balance.)

  WORKED EXAMPLE:
    - Buy in 100 → wallet holds 100, player has 100 credits.
    - Play 8 rounds × 5 = spend 40 → player has 60 credits left.
    - Of the 40 spent: 36 → prize pool (90%), 4 → dev cut (10%).
    - Cash out → player gets 60 onions back to their wallet.
    - Net: player spent 40 real onions to play 8 rounds; 40 remain in the arcade
      wallet (36 pool + 4 dev).

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
6. PRIZE POOL & DEV CUT — POOL MECHANICS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  - Every onion SPENT on gameplay (not deposited, not refunded — spent) is split:
        PRIZE POOL share = floor(spent × 90%)
        DEV CUT share     = spent − pool   (the remaining ~10%)
    The split uses integer math so the two parts always re-sum to the exact
    amount spent (no fractional onion is ever created or lost).
  - The physical onions all sit pooled in the one arcade wallet. The 90/10 split
    is an ACCOUNTING VIEW — it's only "realized" when onions are paid out (a
    prize) or withdrawn (the dev cut).
  - The PRIZE POOL displayed to players (a chip in the top bar) is:
        floor(total_spent × 90%)  −  prizes already paid out
  - The DEV CUT available to withdraw is:
        (total_spent − floor(total_spent × 90%))  −  dev already withdrawn
  - Pool grows automatically as people play. More players / more rounds = bigger
    pot = stronger incentive to compete. It is currently ONE GLOBAL pool across
    all three games (not per-game).

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
7. PAYOUTS / TOURNAMENTS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  - The operator pays prizes from an admin console (secret-gated). Today it is a
    simple form: pick a winner's OnionDAO username + amount → it transfers from
    the arcade wallet to them. The amount is capped at the available prize pool
    (can't over-pay the pool).
  - Intended cadence: a tournament payout ONCE A DAY (operator picks the
    winner(s) from the leaderboards and pays them). This is manual today.
  - The operator separately withdraws the accumulated DEV CUT to their own wallet
    (also admin-gated, capped at the dev-cut balance).
  - There is no automatic prize distribution, no entry-fee tournament brackets,
    and no per-game pots today — all candidates for a redesign.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
8. HOW PLAYERS ARE INCENTIVISED
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  - SKILL competition: the games are pure skill/reflex; better players rank
    higher on permanent leaderboards.
  - REAL STAKES: playing costs real onions, and a real prize pool (90% of all
    onions spent) is paid to top players — so skill converts to real winnings.
  - LOW RISK: a player can cash out unused onions anytime, so they only ever lose
    what they actually played. Buy-in is one approval; play is friction-free.
  - GROWING POT: the pool visibly grows as the community plays, creating a
    flywheel / FOMO to compete before the daily payout.
  WEAKNESSES to consider: no explicit reason to play MORE rounds beyond chasing a
  high score; the pool→winner link is operator-discretion (not transparent/
  automatic); no streaks, achievements, multipliers, or per-game prizes yet.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
9. HOW THE DEVS (OPERATOR) GET PAID
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  - The operator earns a 10% RAKE on every onion SPENT on gameplay (not on
    deposits, and not on the refunded/cashed-out portion — only on onions
    actually played). This accrues continuously and is withdrawable to the
    operator's OnionDAO wallet anytime via the admin console.
  - Example: across a day players collectively spend 10,000 onions on rounds →
    9,000 to the prize pool, 1,000 to the operator.
  - The rake percentage is a single tunable number (currently 10%).

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
10. THE "NO ONIONS LOST" ACCOUNTING MODEL
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
At every instant, every onion in the arcade wallet belongs to exactly one of
four buckets, and they must sum to the wallet's real balance:

    WALLET BALANCE  ==  PLAYER CREDITS (owed back)
                      +  PRIZE POOL (90% of spent, minus prizes paid)
                      +  DEV CUT     (10% of spent, minus dev withdrawn)
                      +  IN-FLIGHT   (transfers started but not yet confirmed)

  - All four are derived by summing an append-only ledger (no mutable counters),
    so they can't silently drift.
  - Money-moving operations (buy-in credit, cash-out, payout, dev-withdraw) are
    exactly-once and atomic: the local debit is committed first, the external
    transfer uses a unique idempotency key, and a transfer is only marked
    complete on confirmed success / only reversed on a definitive rejection /
    left "pending" on a network blip (a reconcile step later settles it).
  - A reconcile check recomputes the equation against the live wallet balance and
    flags any drift (a built-in audit that proves no onions were lost).
  Any redesign MUST preserve an equivalent invariant — it's what guarantees the
  operator never pays out onions that don't exist and never loses player funds.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
11. CURRENT PARAMETERS (the tunable knobs)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
    Cost per round (GAME_COST) .............. 5 onions   (configurable)
    Dev rake ................................ 10%        (→ pool gets 90%)
    Buy-in presets .......................... 25 / 50 / 100 (custom 1–100,000)
    Max single payout / withdrawal .......... 100,000 onions
    Welcome grant (LOCAL mode only) ......... 100 onions (CONNECTED mode = 0)
    Cash-out granularity .................... full balance only (no partial)
    Prize pool scope ........................ ONE global pool (all games share)
    Payout method ........................... manual, single winner, admin form
    Games ................................... 3 (Seven, Lights Out, Onion Chop)

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
12. KEY RULES / EDGE BEHAVIORS (so a redesign doesn't break them)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  - Spent onions are non-refundable (they fund the pool). Only UNUSED credits
    cash out.
  - Free/welcome onions (LOCAL mode) are never cashable as real onions.
  - Deposits require the player's approval; payouts/cash-outs/dev-withdrawals do
    NOT require approval (the arcade authorizes them from its wallet).
  - Points vs tokens: a cash-out/payout to a points user is instant; to a
    badge/token user it can settle asynchronously.
  - Payouts can never exceed the current prize pool; dev-withdrawals can never
    exceed the accrued dev cut.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
13. REDESIGN LEVERS (ideas / open questions to explore)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  - Rake & cost: tune GAME_COST and the 10% rake; dynamic pricing; first-round-
    free; bulk-buy discounts.
  - Pool distribution: automatic & transparent (e.g. top-N split by rank) vs
    manual; per-game pools; scheduled tournaments with start/end + entry; jackpot
    rollover; "winner takes X%, rest split."
  - Engagement: streaks, daily challenges, achievements, combo multipliers that
    affect winnings, seasons/ladders, head-to-head wagers, spectator/side bets.
  - Risk/UX: partial cash-out; auto-cash-out on exit; show expected-value/odds;
    clearer "you've spent X, pool is Y, you rank Z."
  - Fairness/identity: real OnionDAO login to stop leaderboard spoofing; anti-bot;
    per-account limits.
  - Economy safety: keep the four-bucket invariant + reconcile no matter what;
    decide policy for unused pool, refunds, and stuck/async transfers.
